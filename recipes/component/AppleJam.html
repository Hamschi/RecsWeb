Ingredients

  • 750g apples (e.g. Elstar, already prepared)
  • 250g 1:3 gelling sugar (alternatively 375g 1:2 gelling sugar)
  • water (alternatively apple juice)
  • 1TL cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Peel, core, and chop the apples into small pieces
  2. Put apples and lemon juice into a large pot and add 2-3 tbsp water.
  3. Bring water in a water cooker to a boil
  4. Sterilize the jars and their lids just by putting covering them with the boiling water
  5. Simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring, until the apples are soft. If the apples get to dry, add more water to prevent burning
  6. After that: mash well or blend briefly. For a chunky jam, leave pieces as they are
  7. Stir in the gelling sugar thoroughly
  8. Boil and stir the mixture for about 3 minutes
  9. Stir in the cinnamon
  10. adle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving about 5 mm headspace
  11. Wipe rims clean, seal tightly
  12. Put the jars upside down for 5 minutes so that the jam is killing remaining germs at the top of the jar
  13. Let jars cool at room temperature. The jam typically reaches its final thickness after 12–24 hours
  14. Tip: you can basically use any fruits to make jam with this method
