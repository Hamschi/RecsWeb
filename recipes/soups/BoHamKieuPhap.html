Ingredients

  • 500ml water
  • 1tsp salt
  • 400g potatoes
  • 300g carrots
  • 2stalks celery
  • 15g shallot
  • 40g garlic
  • 1pcs onion
  • 1handful parsley
  • 1kg beef
  • 100g shimeji mashroom (alternatively champignon)
  • butter
  • 2tbsp chili sauce (e.g. Chin Su)
  • 200g strained tomatoes
  • 3tbsp oyster sauce
  • 80ml red wine
  • 1.5tbsp chicken stock powder
  • 1tsp garlic powder
  • 4tbsp sugar
  • 1tsp pepper
  • 0.5tsp salt
  • 700ml water
  • 1tbsp chili sauce
  • 10g butter
  • 40ml red wine

Instructions

  1. Add salt to the water
  2. Cut the carrots and celery into 5 cm long sticks (about 1.5 cm thick)
  3. Peel the potatoes and cut into bite-sized pieces
  4. Rinse the potatoes and carrots in the salted water
  5. Cut the onion into wedges
  6. Finely chop the parsley and cut the mushrooms into thirds
  7. Finely chop and blend the garlic and shallots
  8. Cut the beef into slightly larger-than-bite-sized pieces
  9. Heat oil and 2 tbsp butter in a pan
  10. Add the beef and season with garlic powder, salt, and pepper. This may require several batches
  11. Mix salt, chicken stock powder, sugar, oyster sauce, pepper, chili sauce, and 120 g of the strained tomatoes to make the sauce
  12. Melt 40 g butter in a pot and brown the garlic–shallot purée
  13. Add the sauce and stir
  14. Add 80 ml red wine and stir
  15. Add the beef and stir
  16. After about 5 minutes, put 80 g strained tomatoes into a bowl
  17. Add water and mix well
  18. Add the tomato-water mixture to the pot and simmer until the beef becomes tender (about 40 minutes)
  19. Sauté the potatoes and carrots for about 3 minutes and set aside
  20. In the same pan, sauté the onion and celery
  21. After about 2 minutes, add the mushrooms
  22. Once the beef is tender, add the potatoes and carrots
  23. Add chili sauce, butter and then the 40ml wine and simmer for another 5–7 minutes
  24. Add the mushrooms, onions, and celery, then turn off the heat
  25. Garnish with parsley
