80 min.

Ingredients

  • 150g glutinous rice flour
  • 30g corn starch
  • 300ml milk
  • 60g sugar
  • 1g salt
  • 100g mung bean (without skin)
  • 15ml oil
  • 80g sugar
  • 10g corn starch
  • 20ml water
  • food coloring (optional)

Instructions

  1. Put the peeled mung beans into a blender and blend it with a little water until smooth
  2. Transfer the blended mung bean mixture to a pan along with the sugar and the cooking oil, and cook over low heat
  3. Dissolve the cornstarch in 20ml of water, then slowly pour that mixture into the pan of mung beans
  4. Use a spatula to stir continously until the filling thickens, the liquid is absorbed and it forms into a mass
  5. Turn off the heat and let it cool. Roll the mung bean filling into 8 equal balls
  6. Put 30g of cornstarch into a pan and toast over low heat until the cornstarch becomes loose and fluffy. This cornstarch will be used as the coating powder on the outside of the mochi to prevent sticking and make it look nicer
  7. Put the glutinous flour, the sugar and the salt into a bowl along with the milk. Use a whisk to stir until the sugar and flour are completely dissolved
  8. Then, strain the mixture through a sieve once more so that the batter is smooth and free of lumps
  9. Place the bowl of batter into a steamer and steam for about 30 minutes until the dough is cooked
  10. Then transfer the dough onto a kneading mat that has been dusted with the toasted cornstarch
  11. Knead the dough with the cornstarch until it becomes elastic and no longer sticky
  12. Divide the dough into 8 equal portions
  13. Use your hands to press and flatten the mochi dough, then place a mung bean filling ball in the center and wrap it into a round shape.
  14. Tip: To make the mochi more visually appealing, you can add food coloring during the kneading process so that the final product looks more attractive
