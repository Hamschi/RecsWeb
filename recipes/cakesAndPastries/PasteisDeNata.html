Ingredients

  • 140g flour
  • 90g water
  • 1g salt
  • 90g butter (soft spreadable)
  • 20g flour
  • 30g milk
  • 120g milk
  • 5g vanilla paste
  • 80g water
  • 120g caster sugar
  • 1stick cinnamon
  • 3pcs egg yolk

Instructions

  1. Combine the flour, salt, and water to a dough, cover it and let it rest for 30 minutes
  2. Heavily dust the table and the dough with flour
  3. Roll the dough out to a large square using a rolling pin
  4. Brush 2/3 of the dough with the softened butter
  5. Fold the 1/3 of plain dough over and then cover it with the remaining 1/3 of buttered pastry
  6. Roll the pastry out again as much as you can into a square shape
  7. Brush 2/3 of the pastry with the softened butter
  8. Fold the 1/3 of plain pastry over and then cover it with the remaining 1/3 of buttered pastry
  9. Wrap the pastry in clingfilm and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  10. After letting it chill: roll it out to a large square
  11. This time brush the whole surface with butter
  12. Trim the very top and bottom of the pastry and then roll it up into a tight log and pinch the seam together at the end
  13. Wrap in clingfilm and refrigerate for at least an hour
  14. In a small bowl whisk the 20g plain flour with the 30g milk to a smooth paste. Leave on the side
  15. Combine the 120g milk with the vanilla paste in a pan and bring up to a boil
  16. Pour the hot milk over the flour/milk paste and whisk until smooth
  17. Combine the water, sugar, and cinnamon stick in a pan and bring up to a boil
  18. Remove the cinnamon stick and pour the syrup into the bowl with the milk/flour mix. Whisk until combined
  19. Leave the mix to cool down to below 60°C. Then whisk in the egg yolks.
  20. Once you are ready to bake pre-heat the oven to 250C (482F) fan off. Make sure it is well pre-heated for at least 30 minutes
  21. Brush the muffin tin with butter
  22. Trim the edges of the pastry log and cut the pastry into 12 equal pieces
  23. Place the pieces in the muffin tray. Gently press the pastry down and out sideways using your thumbs. You want the bottom to be as thin as possible and the sides to be nice and even. The pastry should cover the whole surface of the muffin mould
  24. Pour the custard mix into the pastry cases and bake for 10 – 12 minutes until the surface of the custard has slightly burned and the sides of the pastry look golden brown
  25. Remove from the muffin tin, leave to cool down.
  26. Tip: This recipe makes 12 small pastéis that fit perfectly in a muffin tin with 6.3cm x 3.8cm moulds.
