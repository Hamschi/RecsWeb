Ingredients

  • 100gbutter cookies
  • 60gbutter
  • 250gcream cheese (double cream)
  • 200gwhip cream
  • 50gyogurt
  • 60gsugar
  • 1.5sheetsgelatin sheets
  • 1dashvanilla extract (alternatively vanilla paste)
  • 7gmatcha powder
  • 30mlmilk (hot)

Preparation

  1. Crush the cookies in a sealed bag into fine crumbs.
  2. Melt the butter and mix evenly with the cookie crumbs.
  3. Place a 15 cm (6 inch) cake ring on a cake plate and form an even base with the crumbs. Don’t press the surface too firmly, or it may become too hard after chilling.
  4. Refrigerate the cookie base for 20 minutes.
  5. Soak the gelatin sheets in water.
  6. Whip the cream with sugar until stiff and refrigerate.
  7. Briefly mix the cream cheese with vanilla extract and yogurt until smooth.
  8. Set aside a small portion of the whipped cream (about one spatula) to dissolve the gelatin later.
  9. Fold the remaining whipped cream gradually into the cream cheese mixture.
  10. Once the gelatin is softened, remove it from the water, gently squeeze it, and place it in a small pot.
  11. Melt the gelatin in the pot over low heat. Once it starts melting, remove from heat to prevent overheating.
  12. When the gelatin is fully melted, mix it with the reserved whipped cream.
  13. Add the gelatin-cream mixture to the rest and stir well.
  14. Take the cookie base from the fridge and spread half of the cream mixture evenly on top.
  15. Place the base back into the fridge.
  16. Dissolve matcha powder in the hot milk until smooth and lump-free.
  17. Once the matcha milk has cooled, mix it into the remaining cream mixture.
  18. When the first cream layer has set in the fridge, evenly spread the green matcha cream on top.
  19. Chill the cake for 3–4 hours in the refrigerator.
  20. Remove the cake ring using a kitchen towel dipped in hot water.
