Ingredients

  • 30g water
  • 55g sugar
  • 30g water
  • 2pcs eggs
  • 25g sugar
  • 240g cream
  • 250g milk

Instructions

  1. Add 30 g water and 55 g sugar to a saucepan and bring to a simmer
  2. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low–medium
  3. When the sugar turns a dark brown caramel color, gently swirl the pan to distribute the color evenly
  4. Turn off the heat and carefully add the remaining 30 g water
  5. The caramel will bubble vigorously—swirl the pan again until fully combined
  6. Evenly divide the liquid caramel among 6 porcelain molds (about 8 × 4 cm)
  7. Add the milk and cream to a saucepan
  8. Heat to about 80 °C (176 °F). Once the mixture begins to steam, turn off the heat
  9. In a bowl, mix the eggs with 25 g sugar until the sugar has fully dissolved
  10. Continue whisking the eggs while gradually adding some of the warm milk-cream mixture to temper them
  11. Preheat the oven to 150 °C (300 °F), top and bottom heat
  12. Place the porcelain molds in a large heatproof dish
  13. Strain the custard mixture through a sieve and evenly pour it into the molds
  14. Cover the molds with aluminum foil
  15. Place the large dish on an oven rack
  16. Heat water to about 80 °C (176 °F) and pour it into the dish until it reaches about two-thirds up the sides of the molds, creating a water bath
  17. Bake for 40–45 minutes
  18. Remove the molds from the water bath and let cool for about 10 minutes
  19. Refrigerate for 1 hour
  20. Run a knife carefully around the edges, invert each mold onto a plate, gently shake to release the caramel sauce, and lift off the mold
