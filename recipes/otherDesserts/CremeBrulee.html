Ingredients

  • 250g whole milk
  • 250g cream (35% fat)
  • 1tbsp vanilla extract
  • 4pcs egg yolk (large)
  • 90g sugar (white)
  • 50g sugar (brown)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 100 °C
  2. Combine the milk and cream in a pot over low heat
  3. Add the vanilla extract and bring the mixture to a boil while stirring continuously
  4. As soon as it starts to simmer, remove the pot from the heat and turn off the stove
  5. Combine the egg yolks with the sugar. Use a wooden spoon instead of a whisk to avoid incorporating air
  6. Once the liquid has cooled slightly, strain one third of it into the egg–sugar mixture
  7. Stir gently until the sugar has dissolved
  8. Strain the remaining liquid into the egg mixture and gently combine again using the wooden spoon
  9. Place the ramekins on a baking tray lined with parchment paper or aluminum foil
  10. Pour the custard evenly through a sieve into the ramekins
  11. Bake the custards at 100 °C for 45–60 minutes. The custard is done when the edges are set but the center still jiggles. Lightly tap the side of the ramekin—if only a slight wobble remains, it’s done
  12. Remove the ramekins from the oven and let them cool for 30–40 minutes
  13. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight
  14. Before serving, sprinkle an even layer of brown sugar on top
  15. Caramelize the sugar using a kitchen torch, by flambéing with rum, or under high oven heat
