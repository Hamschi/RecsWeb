Ingredients

  • 180g chicken breast
  • 0.5tsp tapioca starch (alternatively: corn starch)
  • 1pinch salt
  • 1tbsp water
  • 1drop oil
  • 1tbsp soy sauce (light)
  • 0.5tbsp soy sauce (dark)
  • 2tbsp oyster sauce
  • 0.5tsp sesame oil
  • 1tbsp tapioca starch (alternatively corn starch)
  • 1pinch salt
  • 0.25tsp pepper (white)
  • 4tbsp water
  • 0.5pcs onion
  • 2cloves garlic
  • 1pcs carrot (small)
  • 1handful sugar snap peas
  • 3pcs baby corn
  • 3pcs champignon
  • 1handful mung bean sprouts

Instructions

  1. Slice the carrot thinly, slice the mushrooms, remove the strings from the sugar snap peas, finely chop the onion and garlic, and slice the baby corn diagonally in half
  2. Slice the chicken into thin pieces (about 5 mm thick)
  3. In a bowl, mix the tapioca starch, water, and chicken.
  4. Add one drop of oil, mix again, and set aside
  5. In another bowl, mix the soy sauces, oyster sauce, sesame oil, tapioca starch, salt, pepper, and water to make the sauce
  6. Heat oil in a wok and add the chicken
  7. As soon as the chicken is no longer pink, remove it immediately
  8. Add oil to the wok and sauté the onion and garlic
  9. Fry the carrots in the wok for about 15 seconds
  10. Add the baby corn and sugar snap peas and fry for another 20 seconds
  11. Add the chicken back to the wok along with the mung bean sprouts and mushrooms and fry for about 30 seconds
  12. Stir the sauce once more, pour it into the wok, and mix well
  13. Once the sauce thickens, transfer everything to a plate and serve immediately
